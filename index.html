About Me

I am a final-year PhD researcher specializing in nonlinear optical spectroscopy and molecular-level characterization of interfaces. My research combines Vibrational Sum Frequency Generation (VSFG), Raman spectroscopy, and molecular dynamics simulations to investigate hydrogen bonding, molecular level interaction and interfacial complex chemistry. I have hands-on experience operating and maintaining a laboratory VSFG system, performing routine laser beam alignment, polarization-resolved measurements, spectroscopic data analysis and corroborate experimental findings with computation using GROMACS. I am seeking R&D opportunities in laser technology, optical instrumentation and spectroscopy based laboratories.
